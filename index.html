    • Kinefox tracker
  • People would mostly use it to track their pets.
  • But it is really useful for researchers and scientists who want to track animals in the wild.
  • This technology was invented by Rasmus Worsoe Havmøller with his friends and colleagues.
  • They made a tracker that uses the kinetic energy from the wearer’s movement and converts it to energy to recharge itself.
  • This makes it easier for the scientists and for the animals that are being tracked.
  • Unless the tracker breaks, it can last an animal’s lifetime.
  • They added a low-power GPS tracker, and other technologies into the device and put it into a waterproof and fang-resistant case.
  • The tracking device and collar only weighs 5.3 ounces so small animals can wear it.

    Carbon capture and storage (CCS)
  • It captures the carbon from the atmosphere.
  • The collected carbon will be transported and stored in the rock formations thousands of feet underground.
  • And it can create job opportunities in areas such as construction, maintenance, and engineering.
  • But it is really costly and isn’t really used today.
  • And needs to be improved to reduce environmental impacts.
  • Because there are environmental risks when injecting the carbon into rock formations.
